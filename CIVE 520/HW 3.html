Spatial Variability and Measurement Uncertainty:¶

j. Snotel stations use snow pillows to measure SWE at a point location, usually 3m x 3m. Explain why SWE measurements over such a small area may not be representative of an entire watershed by discussing how i) vegetation, ii) elevation, and iii) aspect can each affect SWE at different locations.¶

SWE measurements from Snotel stations, covering a small area, may not accurately represent an entire watershed due to the influence of vegetation, elevation, and aspect. Dense vegetation can intercept snowfall, leading to reduced snow accumulation on the ground and potential underestimation of SWE, while the type of vegetation can affect the snow's melting rate. For example, trees have a larger canopy than shrubs and that both blocks shortwave radiation from the snow, instead absorbing it and radiating it as longwave radiation down on the snow. This could cause the snow to melt quicker or slower, depending on its albedo and other environmental conditions.

Elevation also significantly influences snowpack dynamics, as higher elevations typically receive more snowfall and can maintain their snowpack for longer due to lower temperatures, which could lead to large variations in SWE in watersheds with signifcant elevation differences. Additionally, the aspect of a slope affects the incident angle of sunlight exposure, which affects the total amount of incident radiation on a slope. This will impact snowmelt rates and cause variations in SWE, with northern aspects generally retaining snow longer in the Northern hemisphere, and southern aspects generally retaining snow longer in the southern hemisphere.

def calc_psy_const(P):
    """
    Calculate psychrometric constant
    c_p: specific heat of dry air at constant pressure in J/kg/K
    P: pressure in Pa
    Lv: latent heat of vaporization in J/kg
    return: psychrometric constant in Pa/°C
    """
    return c_p * P / (epsilon * Lv)

def calc_e_sat(T):
    """
    Calculate saturation vapor pressure
    T: temperature in °K
    Lv: latent heat of vaporization in J/kg
    Rv: gas constant for water vapor in J/kg/K
    return: saturation vapor pressure in Pa
    """
    L_term = np.where(T > 273.15, Lv, Ls)
    return 611 * np.exp((L_term / Rv) * (1 / 273.15 - 1 / T))

def calc_delta(T):
    """
    Calculate slope of the saturation vapor pressure curve
    T: temperature in °C
    Lv: latent heat of vaporization in J/kg
    Rv: gas constant for water vapor in J/kg/K
    return: slope of the saturation vapor pressure curve in Pa/°K
    """
    return (Lv * calc_e_sat(T + Kelvin)) / (Rv * (T + Kelvin)**2) 

def calc_ra(um, z, zd, z0):
    """
    Calculate aerodynamic resistance using constants from Environmentalcrop_constants.
    (TH Eq 22.9)
    :param z: Reference height (m).
    :param zd: Displacement height (m).
    :param z0: Roughness length (m).
    :param um: Wind speed at measurement height (m s-1).
    :param k: von Karman's constant (none). Environmental constant.
    :return: Aerodynamic resistance (s m-1).
    """
    k = 0.4  # von Karman's constant
    return 1 / (k**2 * um) * np.log((z - zd) / z0)**2 

def calc_qs(T, P):
    """
    Calculate saturation specific humidity
    T: temperature in °C
    P: pressure in Pa
    return: saturation specific humidity in kg/kg
    """
    return epsilon * calc_e_sat(T + Kelvin) / P

def calc_q(e, P):
    """
    Calculate specific humidity
    e: vapor pressure in Pa
    P: pressure in Pa
    return: specific humidity in kg/kg
    """
    return epsilon * e / (P - (1 - epsilon) * e)

def calc_rho_a(P, e, T):
    """
    Calculate moist air density
    P: pressure in Pa
    ea: vapor pressure in Pa
    T: temperature in °C
    return: air density in kg/m3
    """
    rho_a = P / (Rd * (T + Kelvin)) + e / (Rv * (T + Kelvin))
    return rho_a

def calc_LH(delta, gamma, Rn, G, rho_a, dq, ra):
    """
    Calculate potential evapotranspiration
    delta: slope of the saturation vapor pressure curve in Pa/°K
    gamma: psychrometric constant in Pa/°C
    Rn: net radiation in W/m2
    G: soil heat flux in W/m2
    rho_a: air density in kg/m3
    dq: vapor pressure deficit in Pa
    ra: aerodynamic resistance in s/m
    return: potential evapotranspiration in m/s
    """
    return ((delta / gamma * (Rn - G)) + ((rho_a * Lv * dq) / ra)) / (1 + delta / gamma)

Environmental Conditions and Calculations¶

Given a set of environmental conditions recorded at station ftc01 on 2023-01-01, we perform calculations to understand various atmospheric and hydrological parameters.

Given Data¶

  • Average Temperature ($T$): $0.89^\circ C$
  • Vapor Pressure ($e$): $499 \, \text{Pa}$
  • Wind Speed ($u_m$): $0.839468 \, \text{m/s}$
  • Net Radiation ($R_n$): $90.14 \, \text{W/m}^2$
  • Pressure ($P_{atm}$): $85000 \, \text{Pa}$
  • Ground Heat Flux ($G$): $0 \, \text{W/m}^2$

Constants¶

  • Latent Heat of Vaporization ($L_v$): $2.5 \times 10^6 \, \text{J/kg}$
  • Specific Heat of Dry Air at Constant Pressure ($c_p$): $1005 \, \text{J/kg/K}$
  • Gas Constant for Dry Air ($R_d$): $287 \, \text{J/kg/K}$
  • Gas Constant for Water Vapor ($R_v$): $461 \, \text{J/kg/K}$
  • Ratio of Gas Constants ($\epsilon$): $\frac{R_d}{R_v}$
  • Characteristic Roughness Height ($z_h$): $0.05 \, \text{m}$
  • Zero Plane Displacement Height ($z_d$): $0.7 \times z_h$
  • Aerodynamic Roughness Length ($z_0$): $0.1 \times z_h$
  • Measurement Height ($z_m$): $2 \, \text{m}$

Calculations¶

Note: $T$ is in $^\circ C$ for all equations below and converted to Kelvin where necessary

Saturation Vapor Pressure¶

$$e_{sat} = 611 \cdot \exp\left(\frac{L_{term}}{R_v} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{273.15} - \frac{1}{T + \text{Kelvin}}\right)\right) = 611 \cdot \exp\left(\frac{2.5 \times 10^6}{461} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{273.15} - \frac{1}{0.89 + \text{Kelvin}}\right)\right) = 651.69 Pa$$

Aerodynamic Resistance ($r_a$)¶

$$ r_a = = \frac{1}{k^2 \cdot u_m} \cdot \ln\left(\frac{z - z_d}{z_0}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{0.4^2 \cdot 0.839} \cdot \ln\left(\frac{2 - 0.035}{0.005}\right)^2 = 265.69 \, \frac{s}{m} $$

Slope of Saturation Vapor Pressure Curve ($\nabla$)¶

$$ \nabla = \frac{L_v \cdot \text{calc\_e\_sat}(T + \text{Kelvin})}{R_v \cdot (T + \text{Kelvin})^2} = \frac{2.5 \times 10^6 \cdot 651.69}{461 \cdot (0.89 + \text{Kelvin})^2} = 47.06 \, \frac{Pa}{^\circ K} $$

Psychrometric Constant ($\gamma$)¶

$$ \gamma = \frac{c_p \cdot P}{\epsilon \cdot L_v} = \frac{1005 \cdot 85000}{\epsilon \cdot 2.5 \times 10^6} = 54.89 \, \frac{Pa}{°C} $$

Saturation Specific Humidity ($q_s$)¶

$$ q_s = \frac{\epsilon \cdot e_{sat}(T + 273.15)}{P_{atm}} = \frac{\frac{R_d}{R_v} \cdot 499}{85000} = 0.00477 \, \frac{kg}{kg} $$

Specific Humidity ($q$)¶

$$ q = \frac{\epsilon \cdot e}{P_{atm} - (1 - \epsilon) \cdot e} = \frac{\frac{R_d}{R_v} \cdot 499}{85000 - (1 - \frac{R_d}{R_v}) \cdot 499} = 0.00366 \, \frac{kg}{kg} $$

Vapor Pressure Deficit ($dq$)¶

$$ dq = q_s - q = 0.00477 - 0.00366 = 0.00111 \, \frac{kg}{kg} $$

Air Density ($\rho_a$)¶

$$ \rho_a = \frac{P_{atm}}{R_d \cdot (T + 273.15)} + \frac{e}{R_v \cdot (T + 273.15)} $$

$$ \rho_a = \frac{85000}{287 \cdot (0.89 + 273.15)} + \frac{499}{461 \cdot (0.89 + 273.15)} = 1.085 \, \frac{kg}{m^3} $$

Latent Heat Flux ($LH$)¶

$$ LH = \frac{\left(\frac{\nabla}{\gamma} \cdot (R_n - G)\right) + \left(\frac{\rho_a \cdot L_v \cdot dq}{r_a}\right)}{1 + \frac{\nabla}{\gamma}} = \frac{\left(\frac{47.06}{54.89} \cdot (90.14 - 0)\right) + \left(\frac{1.085 \cdot 2.5 \times 10^6 \cdot 0.00111}{265.69}\right)}{1 + \frac{47.06}{54.89}} = 47.71 \, \frac{W}{m^2} $$

Potential Evapotranspiration ($PET$)¶

$$ PET = \frac{LH}{L_v \cdot \rho_w} \times 1000 = \frac{47.71}{2.5 \times 10^6 \times 1.085} \times 1000 \times 24 \times 3600 = 1.65 \, \frac{mm}{day} $$
